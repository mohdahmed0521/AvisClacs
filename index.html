Frequently Asked Questions
  • 1. What is the difference between spider veins and varicose veins?
    Both varicose veins and spider veins are veins with damaged valves. The damaged valves in these veins cause the deoxygenated blood to flow backward instead of towards the heart. Spider veins are small varicose veins that are comparatively closer to the skin’s surface. They typically appear in groups and are red or blue in colour.
  • 2. How common is spider veins in India?
    Crores of Indians have spider veins, and the condition is extremely common worldwide. According to a research paper published in 2023 by Dr. Kasuo Miyake, the inventor of the CLaCS procedure, up to 90% of the world’s population develops spider veins.
  • 3. Why is it important to treat spider veins?
    The early treatment of spider veins is crucial for three reasons. Firstly, spider veins can cause symptoms such as irritation, pain, burning and itching. The Cryo Laser and Cryo Sclerotherapy (CLaCS) treatment provides instant relief from the symptoms. Secondly, spider veins make the skin look unpleasant, often making people self-conscious about their looks. Someone with spider veins may limit their clothing choices to try and cover the blue or red veins that look “embarrassing.” Thirdly, the pressure of the backward-flowing blood in the spider veins damages the healthy veins connected to these damaged veins. So, the uncomfortable symptoms can progress to increased pain and swelling over time.
  • 4. Why consult an interventional radiologist over a dermatologist for spider veins treatment?
    Dermatologists are not specifically trained to treat vascular problems, increasing the risk of complications during and after the treatment. The same risk applies if a plastic surgeon performs sclerotherapy. You should trust only the best and most qualified interventional radiologists for spider veins and varicose veins treatment for a fast, painless and risk-free treatment.
